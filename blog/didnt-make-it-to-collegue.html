...for now

I recently received my results from my college applications. I'm not sure how it is in other countries, but in Mexico, you have to take a test to get into public college, which is one of the best options there is. I presented my tests to the UNAM and IPN, which are two of the best universities in Mexico.

Sadly, and against all odds, I didn't make it to either of them. Seeing the results on my phone at 1 a.m. was one of the worst feelings I've ever had. I felt like I had failed my parents, my friends, and most importantly, myself.

I've always wanted to enter one of those universities, and I've always been told that I'm smart enough to do it, so I was really confident that I would make it. I was so confident that I didn't even apply to any other university, and it backfired.

While it was devastating at the moment, it didn't take me long to realize that it wasn't the end of the world. I talked to my parents and I'm very lucky, because they are really supportive. They told me that, even though it was a setback, I could still make it to a good university, and that I could always try again next year.

Fast forward a few months, I'm already studying to apply again. It isn't easy, but I'm trying my best. When I did my tests the first time, I felt so relieved to finally being able to relax, and knowing that I had to do it all over again really puts a lot of pressure on me. I'm trying to stay positive, and I'm trying to study as much as I can, but it's hard.

I hope I can make it this time. I know I will, but I'm still scared. I'm scared of failing again, and I'm scared of disappointing everyone.

I'm sure not a single soul will read this anytime soon, but if you did, thank you. I just needed to vent a little bit.

I will update this or create another post once I have my new results, but until then, thank you for reading.
